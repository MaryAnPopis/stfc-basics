LCARS

Lcars is a discord based service that you can use to query information about missions, ships, tiering costs, hostiles, etc. You can join LCARS here , please learn how to use it as it is the biggest source information for this game.

Daily To-Do List

  • Do all dailies
  • Do at least 2 borg runs
  • Refine materials twice per day
  • Refine inactive nano probes to get charged and active nano probes
  • Refine charged nano probes to faction credits and faction reputation
  • Go to Store > Alliances and exchange your armada credits for resources and armada tactical cores
  • Do at least one Stella run on exchange space
  • Exchange security codes in the rogue faction store
  • Exchange data for directives in the rogue faction store
  • Exchange data for augment credits and reputation on the augment faction store

Mining

This applies mostly to when you start being able to mine and refine 3* materials. Here are the overall tips:
  • Get a North star as soon as you can in the game. While it has low cargo capacity, it is good for fast mining. This ship is worth maxing out.
  • As you start faction locking, make sure to get the faction miners and tier them up to at least tier 4.
  • Always promote Stonn, T'Pring and Barot as soon as you can, also use them as captains of you mining ships based on whatever you are mining.
  • Unless you are doing missions or grinding hostiles with multiple ships at once, keep all your other ships mining.
  • Get all faction miners.

Crews

These are some recommended mining crews based on different purposes (remember that with the exception of the North Star, most people run an empty lower deck on miners):

  • Protected cargo:
    • Joaquin (C), Otto and Kati
    • Joaquin (C), 1 of 10 and T'Pring
    • T'Pring (C), 1 of 10 and someone to give synergy to T'Pring
  • Speed mining:
    • Either T'Pring, Stonn or Barot as captain on their respective faction mining ship, with 10 of 10 and either Cadet Scotty for deep space or Helvia for synergy
    • Either T'Pring, Stonn or Barot as captain, 10 of 10 and Helvia on a North Star with a lower deck of health

Lower decks

STFC does not give you a way to sort officers by stat. You can do the selections manually or use fill up a file like this one with your officer information (make sure they are off ship when you fill it up). If you fill up this file, you will only need to update it when you promote or level officers and if you do research that applies to all officers. This file will give you the opportunity to sort by stat and remove officers you have in other ships when building crews. Focus on the following stats when making crews based on your ship:

  • Explorers: defense
  • Interceptors: attack
  • Battleships: health
  • Miners: health

PVP

PVP is very different than PVE. You have to consider different things like officer level, ship level, target, faction of the target ships, etc. Here are some popular set ups:

  • Gorkon (C), Kerla and Carol on a D4 with lower deck of attack
  • Kang (C), Mara and 8 of 10 on a Saladin with lower deck of attack
  • Kirk (C), Nero and Decius on an Enterprise with lower deck of defense

Hostile hotspots

To find systems suitable for grinding hostiles, you can use commands like the ones below:

  • !hostiles int <level-needed-here>
  • !hostiles exp <level-needed-here>
  • !hostiles bs <level-needed-here>

Leveling Up

This section should be considered as advice and not general rules, most of these things are meant to make leveling up more efficient.

Ops

This is very simple. Never upgrade Ops until you have completed all buildings and research for your current level. Combat, mining and a lot of other things are greatly influenced by buildings and research. Also, if you upgrade Ops, your dailies increase. You can check the dailies for a given level using this LCARS command:

  • !daily

Faction locking

While doing missions or killing hostiles in Federation, Romulan or Klingon space, you can increase or decrease your reputation to these factions. The better the reputation, the more options you have in the faction store to get things like recruit tokens or ship blueprints. Here are a few facts:

  • If your reputation to a faction goes below 0, every time you enter a system by that faction, your ship will get a chaser (a hostile ship chasing yours). Faction store also gets closed and there are no dailies for that faction.
  • If you get to 10 million, the value will never go down from that (AKA, faction has been locked). This opens the possibility to start getting prints for your first epic ship (Enterprise, D4 and Augur).
  • It is generally recommended to lock factions 1 at a time, however you could theoretically do 2, but it would take a lot of time.

As soon as you start getting missions that give you faction reputation, choose a faction and archive the missions that increase your reputation for the other factions. Here are some tips for locking the first one:

  • Always do your faction dailies.
  • Always hit faction transports instead of any other ship, transports usually give 2 times or more reputation.
  • To do your dailies, choose the appropriate faction to kill hostiles for:
    • If you choose the Federation, hit Klingons.
    • If you choose Klingons, hit Romulans.
    • If you choose Romulans, hit the Federation.
  • Always join the daily reputation event. If you feel you cannot finish it, you can donate the necessary resources to your alliance, and it will count towards the event.
  • Add an additional “daily” to go get some Inert Nanoprobes, refine them into Charged Nanoprobes and convert them to reputation points. You can do this daily.
  • Kill as many hostiles of the highest level you can per day.
